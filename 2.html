Daintree Forest

The Daintree Rainforest is a region on the northeast coast of Queensland, Australia, north of Mossman and Cairns.
At around 1,200 square kilometres (460 sq mi) the Daintree is a part of the largest continuous area of tropical rainforest on the Australian continent.

The Daintree Rainforest is a part of the Wet Tropics of Queensland Rainforest, that spans across the Cairns Region. The Wet Tropics Rainforest (that the Daintree is a part of) is the oldest continually surviving tropical rainforest in the world. Along the coastline north of the Daintree River, tropical forest grows right down to the edge of the sea.
